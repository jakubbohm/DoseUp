Jakub responds well to structured, option-based interview rounds (AskUserQuestion, ~4 questions each) for requirements gathering, and he engages beyond the offered options. When he picks an offered option (in any tool's multiple choice), the pick is only the *closest match*, not his words or full agreement — only his free-text notes are his own; re-confirm before recording a picked option as a verdict, and never quote option text as his (design-prompt steering, 2026-07-18: he rejected an embedded 'breadth over polish' trade-off he had ostensibly picked — his actual priority is breadth *and* best quality; long-term usability outranks time/token savings). He makes firm technology decisions and will override recommendations (chose React + Vite over recommended Blazor; wrote in Cosmos DB as a custom answer, then reversed it himself to Neon Postgres a day later — the recorded database reversal, ADR-0001-platform-and-stack). He may revisit his own decisions pre-implementation; record reversals, don't resist them. He treats the DoseUp repo as a showcase of best practices, conventions, and Claude-assisted workflow — process and documentation quality are first-class goals, see [[memory-in-repo]] and [[user-gates-stage-progression]].
